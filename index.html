Frequently Asked Questions

What makes PlanivestFin different from other calculators?

Most calculator sites give you one calculator at a time. PlanivestFin is the only tool that lets you enter all your investments — SIPs, Lumpsum, FDs, Gold, Silver, and NPS — simultaneously and gives you a unified wealth picture with combined charts and total corpus value.

How accurate are the SIP calculations?

Our SIP calculator uses the mathematically correct formula: the periodic rate is derived using (1 + annual rate)^(1/frequency) - 1, which accounts for proper compounding. This matches the industry standard used by SEBI-registered mutual fund calculators.

What is a Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)?

A SIP is a method of investing in mutual funds where a fixed amount is invested at regular intervals. This approach helps average out the cost of investment over time (rupee cost averaging) and is a disciplined way to build long-term wealth.

How is the FD maturity value calculated?

We use the standard compound interest formula: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt) where P is the principal, r is the annual rate, n is the compounding frequency per year, and t is the tenure. You can choose monthly, quarterly, half-yearly or annual compounding.

What is the National Pension System (NPS)?

NPS is a voluntary, long-term retirement-focused investment scheme regulated by PFRDA. Contributions are invested in a mix of equity and debt, and a minimum 40% of the final corpus must be used to purchase an annuity for a monthly pension after age 60.

Are the Gold and Silver rates live?

Yes. We fetch live gold and silver prices in INR on page load so you can reference current market rates. The default return rate in the calculator is an estimate based on historical averages — you can adjust it to match your own expectations.
